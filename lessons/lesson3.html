Now maybe you want a little more control over each dot of light.

Here's a new function: colorpixel.

colorpixel will turn on one dot - called a "pixel" - in the color of your choice. To do this, it needs to know 5 things:

  1. The row your pixel is in (count down!)
  2. The column your pixel is in (count across!)
  3. How much red light (remember, between 0 and 255)
  4. How much blue light
  5. How much green light

Try this code out: 

colorpixel (row: 2, column: 3, red: 100, green: 0, blue: 100)

Do you see a pixel that is not like the others? Now try changing the rows, columns, and colors to see what happens. You can even try more than one instruction, one after the other. Just put each one on its own line.

What happens if you upload this code? 

colorpixel (row: 10, column: 3, red: 100, green: 0, blue: 100)

Nothing! There are only 5 rows, and that code tries to turn on a pixel in row 10 - which doesn't exist!
